SECTION 1: LEGAL AUTHORITY

PURPOSE:

To outline the legal authority of the San Andreas State Police.

POLICY:

The San Andreas State Police derives its legal authority from the San Andreas State Constitution and the San Andreas Penal Code. State Troopers are sworn to support the Constitution of the United States and uphold the laws of the State of San Andreas in accordance with the Legislative, Judicial, and Executive branches of the San Andreas State Government.

PROCEDURE:

The State Commissioner of Police is a constitutional officer as per the San Andreas State Constitution, Article IV, Section 1. State Troopers have criminal and civil jurisdiction to enforce state laws and support local ordinances.

JURISDICTION:

Jurisdiction is restricted by law or policy. The San Andreas State Police maintain civil and concurrent criminal jurisdiction on state and federal-owned properties, including various areas such as highways and penitentiaries. Civil jurisdiction extends to cities, townships, or properties not owned by the state or federal governments.

SECTION 2: CODE OF CONDUCT

PURPOSE:

To establish a uniform code of conduct among all San Andreas State Troopers.

PROCEDURE:

All State Troopers will uphold a demeanor of friendliness, professionalism and approachability befitting an officiated member of law enforcement representing the State Police, at all times, while on duty. No State Trooper will obey or carry out any order or command that is unlawful. The chain of command of the State Police will be adhered to at all times. (Section 4 of this document pertains to the chain of command.) All State Troopers are expected to retain knowledge of their training (especially the Use of Force Continuum) as well as the contents of this S.O.P. at all times, and conduct themselves within the scope of both. State Troopers are expected to lead by example and set the standard for their team. Upon joining the department, SD-7 Probationary Troopers will complete a training program and evaluation conducted and mandated by the department’s Field Training Program. Failure to complete the program with satisfactory scores will result in a re-training and/or non-hire. The High Command (administrative) team of the San Andreas State Police reserves the right to add, retract, alter, or amend any of the content within this document at any time, with notice.

PENALTIES:

10-71 Supervisors reserve the right to dismiss insubordinate troopers from an active scene or from duty for a determined period of time. Repeated offenses will lead to an escalation in consequences up to and including termination of employment from the department. State Troopers will not shirk duties, enter bars/taverns, theaters or other such establishments, or attend public entertainment except in order to further fulfill job duties. Troopers wearing a uniform or any clothing that identifies them as an officer of the San Andreas State Police will not gamble or consume alcoholic beverages, especially on duty. Penalties range from suspension to termination of employment from the department. The duties performed by the San Andreas State Police are vital to the safety and well-being of the public. It is essential that all troopers maintain a high standard of conduct. Any witnessed or reported misconduct will be thoroughly investigated by High Command. The administrative team of the department aforementioned reserves the right to suspend any trooper from duty during an ongoing investigation if deemed necessary.

SECTION 3: ANTI-CORRUPTION CAMPAIGN

PHILOSOPHY

As an extension to our policy regarding misconduct, the San Andreas State Police in conjunction with The Blaine County Sheriff’s Office and the Los Santos Police Department have signed a joint agreement to create a staunch policy against illegal/unlawful activities conducted by law enforcement officials including corruption and collusion with any form of criminal element, or organized crime. As an active duty law enforcement officer, affiliating yourself with criminals not only poses a severe security risk to yourself and your team members, but also poses a severe security threat to the public’s well-being. At best, you are putting your livelihood and career at risk; at worst, you are putting your entire life on the line. It simply isn’t worth it. Anyone suspected or accused of colluding with organized crime will be placed on an indefinite suspension from duty until an Internal Affairs investigation is completed. Anyone found guilty of corruption or collusion with a criminal organization will be terminated from the S.A.S.P., and a warrant will be issued for their arrest. Information will be forwarded to the Department of Justice as well as any relevant Federal authorities. **GOALS:** - To eliminate the presence and possibility of illegal activity and corruption within the S.A.S.P. **OBJECTIVES:** - To reduce if not eliminate the grip of organized crime families and syndicates on law enforcement officials within the State of San Andreas. - To tackle organized crime as part of an ongoing series of policies, investigations and tactics to thwart crime and uphold public safety.

SECTION 4: CHAIN OF COMMAND

PURPOSE:

To outline enlisted ranks, their responsibilities, and the chain of command in order from bottom-to-top of the chain.

PROCEDURE:

The chain of command is a chain of authority and accountability from the highest position to the lowest element, with the goal being that individuals receive instructions or guidance from only one supervisor one rank above them. The chain of command from top to bottom is as follows:

Rank Description
Commander Has authority over the entire department, including documents. There will be a Deputy Commander with the same responsibilities.
Lieutenant Oversees all duty and patrol operations within the department, with control over all patrolling activities. Responsible for reviewing and approving demotions proposed by Sergeants.
Sergeant Patrols, oversees lower levels, promotes Troopers to Trooper 1st class, supervises lower ranking officers, has the authority to remove Troopers if necessary (does not apply to Trooper 1st Class), and can propose demotions to the Lieutenant.
Trooper 1st Class Responsible for patrolling and accepting new applicants.
Trooper The base level rank responsible for patrolling and protecting citizens.

The San Andreas State Police’s administrative team reserves the right to demote, promote, or dismiss any person(s)by means of professional discretion.

SECTION 5: VEHICLE POLICIES

PURPOSE:

To outline protocols for choosing and driving department-owned vehicles in a manner that is practical and effective.

PROCEDURE:

Consult the SASP Vehicle Structure document for information on the San Andreas State Police patrol fleet. Any detention of an individual, including any arrests, must be conducted with a “marked” vehicle. State Troopers shall not under any circumstances discharge their firearm at or from a moving vehicle unless an occupant of a fleeing vehicle is using deadly force against them or another person. This includes if an occupant is attempting to use the vehicle to cause bodily injury or death to the deputy or another person. PIT (Pursuit Intervention Technique) maneuvers may be conducted during a 10-80 in which the vehicle occupant is refusing to stop or comply and is recklessly driving. PITs may only be performed by vehicles equipped with a push-bar in clement weather, with speeds under 80 MPH, and with the approval of a 10-71 as a successful PIT may cause accidental bodily injury or death of the occupant. If a pursuit is to exceed 150 MPH, all units are to immediately disengage the pursuit and initiate a BOLO on the vehicle. Vehicle pursuits at or above this speed have been deemed too unsafe to intervene by order of the San Andreas State Police command team.

SECTION 6: UNIFORMS (MALE)

PURPOSE:

To establish a clean and professional uniform code among members of the department.

PROCEDURE:

All State Troopers will adhere to uniform guidelines laid out below while on duty. Any variations that differ from the uniform codes cataloged below will need to be approved by a Lieutenant or higher.

Uniform Description
Commander Commanders are required to wear the highest level of uniform, which includes a formal dress uniform with a hat and badges.
Lieutenant Lieutenants are expected to wear a formal dress uniform with badges, but the hat is optional.
Sergeant

Sergeants can choose between two options:

  • Standard uniform with badges and a hat (optional).
  • Long-sleeve uniform with badges and a hat (optional).
Trooper 1st Class Troopers 1st Class are required to wear a standard uniform with badges, but the hat is optional.
Trooper Troopers should wear the standard uniform with badges and a hat.
